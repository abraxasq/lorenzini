There is a way of moving through a place that resembles skimming — airport, hotel lobby, famous restaurant, airport again. You have technically been somewhere. The passport says so.

And there is another kind of movement: the kind where you wake up in the same neighborhood for the fourth morning, learn which café opens earliest, and start to recognize the delivery driver's route.

The second kind is slower. It produces less content. It is harder to summarize at dinner parties.

It is also the only kind that leaves a mark on you.

## The Parallel in Reading

I read this way too, badly. I have stacked half-finished books across two apartments and three decades, visited them like tourist attractions and moved on.

The books that changed me are the ones I lived in for a while. Not always the longest ones — sometimes a short book, read three times. Sometimes a chapter returned to until the argument became reflex.

Exploration is not motion. It is attention held long enough to let a place teach you something it was not advertising.
