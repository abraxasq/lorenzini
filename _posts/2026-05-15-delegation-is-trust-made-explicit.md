Poor delegation sounds like: *handle this.* Good delegation sounds like: *here is what done looks like, here is the decision you have authority to make, here is the moment I need to be looped in.*

The difference is not style. It is whether the other person has enough information to succeed without you.

## The Failure Mode

Most delegation failures are spec failures. The work comes back wrong not because the person was incompetent but because the picture in your head was never transmitted. You said *handle this* and meant a very specific thing, and they handled it according to a different and equally reasonable interpretation.

This is not their failure. It is yours, as the person who held the complete model and did not share it.

## What Explicit Trust Requires

Genuine delegation requires you to:

1. Be specific about outcomes (not process — outcomes)
2. Define the authority boundary explicitly
3. Resist the urge to check in before the agreed checkpoint
4. Accept that the method may differ from yours without the result being worse

The last one is the hardest. Micromanagement is often perfectionism at a lower altitude — the manager who cannot tolerate watching someone else's process even when the output is fine.
