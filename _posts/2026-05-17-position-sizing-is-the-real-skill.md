Most market participants spend the majority of their intellectual energy on entry: when to buy, what to buy, how to identify the opportunity. Position sizing — how much to buy — receives a fraction of the same attention.

This is backwards.

## Why Sizing Dominates Returns

A good idea at 0.5% of the portfolio contributes 5bps to your return if it doubles. A mediocre idea at 10% of the portfolio contributes 1000bps if it doubles and loses 1000bps if it goes to zero.

The return distribution of a portfolio is shaped more by how you weight your positions than by which positions you select. This is not an argument for random stock selection — the quality of ideas still matters. But it means that two investors with identical idea generation but different sizing discipline can have dramatically different outcomes.

## The Sizing Framework I Use

I think about positions in tiers:

- **Core (5–10%):** High conviction, well-understood, comfortable holding through a 30% drawdown
- **Standard (2–4%):** Good ideas where my edge is real but the uncertainty is higher
- **Speculative (<1%):** Asymmetric scenarios I want exposure to but cannot size confidently

The core tier is where I do the most work. I do not put a position there until I have answered the question: what would have to be true for me to be wrong, and how would I know?
