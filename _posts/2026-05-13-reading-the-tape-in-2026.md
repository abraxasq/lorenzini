The tape still tells a story. The medium has changed — we read Level 2 quotes and time-and-sales on a screen rather than watching a paper ribbon — but the underlying information is similar: who wants to own this at what price, and who wants to sell.

The skill that has not changed is attention. You cannot read what you are not watching, and what you watch is a choice that determines everything downstream.

## What I Am Watching

This quarter, a few things are holding my attention:

**Credit spreads relative to equity vol.** When these diverge — when equity looks complacent and credit looks cautious — the credit market is usually the one that knows something.

**Sector rotation pace.** Fast rotation is often noise. Slow, sustained rotation — money moving out of one sector over weeks, not hours — tends to reflect a real re-rating of risk or opportunity.

**Small-cap relative strength.** Small caps are a risk-on/risk-off barometer with a long track record. When they underperform persistently, the risk appetite signal is worth taking seriously.

None of these is a trading system. They are reference points — things to be aware of before forming a view, not conclusions in themselves.
