Every good map starts with acknowledging the blank space. The cartographer does not pretend to know the terrain before walking it — she marks what is known, names what is uncertain, and leaves white where the record stops.

That is the first discipline of exploration: intellectual honesty about the edges.

## The Three Moves

When entering unknown territory, I have found three moves reliable:

1. **Name what you already know.** Lay it out flat. The familiar is the baseline from which deviation becomes legible.
2. **Find one local guide.** Not a comprehensive expert — just someone who has been here before, who can say *watch the third step* and save you the fall.
3. **Budget for revision.** Your first map will be wrong. Plan for at least two iterations before committing to the route.

The cartographer does not fail when the terrain contradicts the map. She updates the map.

> The territory always wins. The map is just your current best guess.

This is the correct relationship between model and reality — and the explorer who forgets it gets lost.
