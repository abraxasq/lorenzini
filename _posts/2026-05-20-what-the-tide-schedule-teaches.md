The tide schedule is one of the few genuinely predictable things in the world. You can consult it a year in advance and be right to the minute. The sea does not have opinions about your plans.

I keep a printed tide schedule in my desk drawer even when I am nowhere near the ocean. It is a reminder that some rhythms are fixed, and that the intelligent response to a fixed rhythm is to align with it rather than fight it.

## On Timing and Systems

The explorer who ignores the tide does not demonstrate independence. She demonstrates ignorance of the system she is moving through.

There are tides in markets, in organizations, in creative work, in the body's own chemistry. Most of them are less predictable than the ocean's, but most of them are more predictable than we admit.

The discipline I am slowly learning is to check the tide schedule before launching the boat — to distinguish between conditions I can change and conditions that are simply there, asking to be read correctly.
